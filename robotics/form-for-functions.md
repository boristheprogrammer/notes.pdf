# Form for functions

## Why?

In most science fiction stories, robots are humanoids. They have the same look,
the same shape and even the same constraints that humans have.

But why do we think that the most advanced robots would ever look like a human being in the first place?
Some say it is for better server humans and for that I kinda of agree.
Others say that it is for humans to feel at ease when confronted with all those robots moving all over the place and for that I also agree.
But to think that all robots will be humanoids is not a good idea in my opinion.

We still need to ask the Why when building each individual machine.
In my opinion, each robots should be design with a specific purpose in mind.
And in this short article, I would like to analyze why we need to keep each robots with its form matching,
or more appropriate for the function that robot will serve.

## Tiny or Big

One example of robots we can think of is those relatively tiny robot vaccum cleaner.
Nobobdy would ever argue about the shape of that robot simply because its shape correspond to its function.
If we had to have a humanoid instead to do the same job, it would look weird and cumbersome.
But maybe that example is a bit out of place, but you get the idea.

Other examples could be seen in other type of machines for example heavy machines such as excavator machines.
Nobody would replace those machines with humanoids regardless of their number.

So you get the idea, whether they are tiny or big, robots or machine in general are made for a specific purpose not
just to look like humans but to perform a specific function the best they are designed.
